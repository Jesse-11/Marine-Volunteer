Support marine life where it needs you most.

Join practical volunteer programs that protect Australian coastlines, care for sea animals, and keep local conservation projects moving.

Marine Research

Support field surveys, species monitoring, and citizen science projects led by conservation teams.

Animal Rehabilitation

Help care teams prepare supplies, transport resources, and support recovery spaces for injured wildlife.

Ocean Cleanup

Join beach and reef cleanup days that remove plastic, fishing line, and harmful debris from habitats.

Reef Restoration

Contribute to planting, reporting, and education programs that help restore vulnerable reef systems.

Animal Welfare

Promote safer coastlines through outreach, local events, and responsible marine-life education.

Marine Publication

Help turn research and rescue stories into accessible articles, guides, and campaign materials.

Tidekeepers Collective supports thousands of Australian marine welfare actions every single year!

Our volunteers help transform local concern into practical conservation work, from shoreline cleanup to rescue support and public education.

35,240

Animals cared for

In 2022-2023 Tidekeepers Collective cared for over 30,000 marine species including sharks, dolphins, fish, dugong, birds and more. Excluding any introduced wild animals.

10,612

Cruelty Cases Investigated

In 2022-2023 Tidekeepers Collective received almost 15,000 welfare reports from South Australian sources, thoroughly investigating over 10,000 of them.

179

Marine Articles Published

Tidekeepers Collective supports Australian marine research by publishing accessible conservation resources.
